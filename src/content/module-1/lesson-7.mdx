## 1. üìò Deep Theory: Objective vs. Subjective

Every German Modal Verb has two faces:

1. **Objective (Normal):** Facts, Rules, Ability.
* *Er muss arbeiten.* (He has a contract, he is obliged).


2. **Subjective (Speaker's Opinion):** Guessing, Assessing, Hearsay.
* *Er muss zu Hause sein.* (The light is on. I am 99% sure he is there).



---

## 2. üìä The Scale of Probability (Vermutung)

When you guess how likely something is, you choose the modal verb according to your certainty percentage.

1. **m√ºssen (99% - Almost certain):** Logical conclusion.
* *Das Licht brennt. Er **muss** zu Hause sein.*


2. **m√ºssten / d√ºrften (75-90% - Very probable):** Careful assumption.
* *Der Zug **d√ºrfte** gleich kommen.* (It is usually on time).


3. **k√∂nnen / k√∂nnten (50% - Possible):** 50/50 chance.
* *Es **k√∂nnte** morgen regnen.*


4. **m√∂gen (Concession):** Often used to admit a fact before contradicting it ("He may be rich, but...").
* *Er **mag** recht haben, aber ich glaube ihm nicht.*

## 3. üó£Ô∏è The Hearsay Verbs: Sollen vs. Wollen

This is the most important part for Goethe B2 Reading.

### A. Sollen (Others say...)

Used when reporting rumors, news, or general opinion. You distance yourself from the statement.

* **Meaning:** "It is said that...", "Reportedly...", "Supposedly..."
* *Example:* *Der neue Film **soll** sehr gut sein.* (Critics say it is good. I haven't seen it).

### B. Wollen (Self-Claim)

Used when someone claims something about themselves, but implies some doubt or lack of proof.

* **Meaning:** "He claims to be...", "He pretends to..."
* *Example:* *Der Zeuge **will** den T√§ter gesehen haben.* (He says so, but maybe he is lying).

## 4. ‚è≥ The Grammar of the Past (Subjective Past)

How do you express a guess about the past?
**Structure:** Modal Verb (Present) + **Infinitive Perfect** (ge-form + haben/sein).

* *Present:* Er **muss** zu Hause **sein**. (He must be home now).
* *Past:* Er **muss** gestern zu Hause **gewesen sein**. (He must have been home yesterday).
* *Present:* Er **soll** das Geld **stehlen**. (People say he steals).
* *Past:* Er **soll** das Geld **gestohlen haben**. (People say he stole).
