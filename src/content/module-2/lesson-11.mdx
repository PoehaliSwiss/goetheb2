## 1. üìò Deep Theory: The Distance Marker (Konjunktiv I)

In German, when you report what someone else said without guaranteeing it's true, you use **Konjunktiv I**. It signals: *"I am just the messenger."*

### The Formation (The "e" rule)

Take the **Infinitive Stem** and add **-e, -est, -e, -en, -et, -en**.

* *sein:* ich sei, du seiest, **er sei**, wir seien, ihr seiet, **sie seien**.
* *haben:* er **habe**.
* *k√∂nnen:* er **k√∂nne**.
* *wollen:* er **wolle**.

### The "Substitution" Rule (The Safety Net)

This is the most critical rule for exams.
**If Konjunktiv I looks exactly like the Normal Present Tense (Indikativ), you must switch to Konjunktiv II.**

* *Scenario:* "Sie sagen" (They say).
* *K1:* "Sie sagen, sie **haben** Zeit." (Looks like normal Present -> Ambiguous).
* *Switch:* "Sie sagen, sie **h√§tten** Zeit." (Konjunktiv II used as substitute).

**Rule of Thumb:**

* **Singular (er/sie/es):** Almost always use **Konjunktiv I** (*er habe, er sei, er wisse*).
* **Plural (sie/wir):** Almost always switch to **Konjunktiv II** (*sie h√§tten, sie w√§ren*), because K1 (*sie haben*) looks invisible.
